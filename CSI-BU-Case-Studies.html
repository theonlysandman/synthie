Partnership goals and status

Four shared goals guide the CSI-Microsoft partnership: revenue growth, purposeful modernization, employee productivity, and trust through security.

Goal 1

Unlock AI revenue

Help CSI companies package AI into differentiated product capability, premium features, new offerings, and customer value that can expand revenue.

Goal 2

Modernize with purpose

Prioritize modernization that clearly drives revenue and/or reduces cost through better platforms, lower infrastructure debt, and faster product delivery.

Goal 3

Support CSI employees

Equip CSI teams to evolve the way they work so they can ship, sell, and support faster and better with Microsoft tools, AI, and repeatable practices.

Goal 4

Secure products and people

Reduce risk and build customer trust by strengthening the security posture of CSI products, platforms, employees, and customer-facing operations.

MACC mechanics

What typically decrements the Microsoft Azure Consumption Commitment

As a working rule, eligible Azure consumed services reduce the MACC when they are billed against the enrolled agreement. The account team should confirm eligibility for each workload and offer before commitments are counted.

Usually counts when eligible

  • Core Azure consumption: compute, storage, networking, databases, security, monitoring, backup, and related Azure platform services.
  • Azure AI, Azure OpenAI, Fabric capacity, and data services when purchased and billed as eligible Azure consumption.
  • Some Azure Marketplace purchases if the offer is MACC-eligible and transacted through the right billing path.
  • Reserved instances, savings plans, and committed Azure capacity when applied to eligible Azure services.

Often does not count unless explicitly eligible

  • Microsoft 365, Dynamics 365, standalone GitHub, support, consulting, hardware, training, and most professional services.
  • Third-party SaaS or Marketplace offers that are not marked MACC-eligible.
  • Services billed outside the enrolled Azure agreement or outside the CSI-approved commitment structure.
  • Licensing benefits that reduce cost but are not Azure consumption themselves.
Recommended BU path: start with a 30-60 minute opportunity triage, classify the workload as migrate, modernize, AI, data, or productivity, confirm MACC eligibility with the Microsoft account team, then attach the right funding and specialist motion before a BU commits delivery resources.
